The lab website now hosts an [**interactive animation of the standing-wave
thermoacoustic mechanism**](/en/research/animations/standing-wave/),
following the schematic concepts in the thermoacoustics textbooks of
G. W. Swift and T. Biwa. You can switch between three regimes —
**uniform**, **refrigerator**, and **engine** — to observe damped spirals,
counter-clockwise closed orbits, and clockwise closed orbits in the phase
space.

The animation synchronizes the gas-parcel oscillation, the
pressure–displacement / pressure–volume trajectories, the temperature–position
trajectory, the 3D `(p, S, ξ)` orbit, and its three 2D projections — best
read together with the [overview of the thermoacoustic effect](/en/research/thermoacoustic-effect/).
