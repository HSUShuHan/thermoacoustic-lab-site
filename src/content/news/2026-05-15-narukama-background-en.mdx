The lab homepage now features an animated narukama (鳴釜) backdrop as its
visual anchor. The narukama is among the earliest documented thermoacoustic
phenomena, recorded in 1568 in the *Tamonin Nikki* of Kofuku-ji, Nara, where
the kettle ritual at Kibitsu Shrine in Bicchū is described (see Fig. 1 and
the boxed diary passage on the
[thermoacoustic effect page](/en/research/thermoacoustic-effect/)).
