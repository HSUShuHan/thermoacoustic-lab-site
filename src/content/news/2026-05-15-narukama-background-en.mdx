The lab homepage now features an animated narukama (鳴釜) backdrop as its
visual anchor. The narukama is among the earliest documented thermoacoustic
phenomena, recorded in 1568 in the *Tamonin Nikki* of Kofuku-ji, Nara, where
the kettle ritual at Kibitsu Shrine in Bicchū is described (see Fig. 1 and
the boxed diary passage on the
[thermoacoustic effect page](/en/research/thermoacoustic-effect/)).
The animated SVG depicts rice grains inside the steamer, boiling water with
rising bubbles, breathing flames, steam wisps, and plane sound wavefronts
radiating from the open end — overlaid with a continuous small-scale
displacement that conveys the acoustic resonance.

The narukama is fixed to the viewport so it persists during scroll; the
header is sticky at the top and content cards float in front. On mobile the
narukama is centred and rendered in grayscale to reduce visual competition
with the text.
