We added an [**interactive "standing & traveling waves in a gas column" animation**](/en/research/animations/air-column/)
as the acoustics primer for the [standing-wave thermoacoustic mechanism animation](/en/research/animations/standing-wave/).

Rendered live on a `<canvas>`, it shows gas parcels oscillating longitudinally to form compressions and rarefactions.
Switch between **traveling**, **standing**, and **superposition** modes, compare **closed–closed / open–open / closed–open**
boundaries and their harmonics, and read the displacement, volume-flow-rate and pressure waveforms, the resonance spectrum,
the complex-plane phasors, and the $p'$–$u'$ and $p'$–$\xi'$ trajectories together.

The page also carries the **full mathematical thread**: the complex representation and Euler's formula
($\mathsf{a}'=\mathrm{Re}\{\mathsf{A}\,e^{i\omega t}\}$), the traveling/standing/superposition pressure field,
wavenumber and angular frequency $k=2\pi/\lambda=\omega/c$ (with a space–time waterfall figure), and the key point —
the phase difference $\Delta\varphi$ between pressure and volume flow rate, set by the argument of the acoustic
impedance $Z=P/U$: in phase for a traveling wave, $90^\circ$ apart for a standing wave — exactly what separates
traveling-wave from standing-wave thermoacoustic engines.

We also unified the notation across the thermoacoustic animations (a prime for time-domain quantities, uppercase for
complex amplitudes, Swift's $e^{+i\omega t}$ convention, acoustic impedance and volume flow rate) and applied it back to
the [standing-wave mechanism animation](/en/research/animations/standing-wave/).
