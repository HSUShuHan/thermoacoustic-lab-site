For fiscal year 2026 we received two grants, [both now listed on the projects page](/en/projects/).

The NSTC individual research project "Research on Hybrid Non-Hermitian Acoustic Metamaterials Based on Thermoacoustic Bias and Electroacoustic Switching", led by Shu-Han Hsu, runs three years (August 2026 to July 2029). It brings the self-excited gain of a thermoacoustic engine into acoustic metamaterials, using thermoacoustic bias and electroacoustic switching to break reciprocity and studying non-reciprocal sound transport under non-Hermitian conditions.

The Ministry of Education Teaching Practice Research project "Implementing Hands-on Fluidyne Stirling Engine Practice in Heat Engines or Advanced Thermodynamics Courses" is a one-year grant. It brings hands-on fabrication and measurement of a liquid-piston (Fluidyne) Stirling engine into the Heat Engines course, letting students compare abstract thermodynamic cycles against measured data.
