## 計畫概述

**關鍵詞**：聲學超材料、非厄米聲學、熱聲效應、主動控制、Hatano-Nelson 模型、趨膚效應

傳統聲學系統受限於互易性原理，難以實現聲波的單向傳輸與拓樸保護。現有技術中，熱聲裝置雖具備高能量密度的被動非互易性，但受限於熱慣性而缺乏即時調控能力；純電聲主動控制雖響應快速，卻在大尺度應用下面臨高功耗與訊號失真之瓶頸。為突破上述限制，本計畫提出一種創新之「熱聲偏置、電聲開關」混合式非厄米聲學超材料架構。

本研究旨在結合熱聲效應之寬頻流體增益（$\tau$）與電聲回授之靈活邊界控制（$Z_{\mathrm{active}}$），建構雙源驅動之聲學 Hatano-Nelson 晶格系統。理論方面，本計畫基於傳遞矩陣，建立連續聲學參數與離散拓樸模型跳躍振幅間之精確映射；實驗方面，將利用 FPGA 壓控電流源技術整合高溫熱聲核心，驗證「以熱供能、以電控相」之高效控制策略。

本計畫預期能成功觀測到由軸向溫度梯度所驅動之動態非厄米趨膚效應，並開發出具備毫秒級切換能力的次波長低頻吸音與聲學邏輯元件，為拓樸聲學與噪音防制工程開闢具高能源效率之新技術可能。

## Abstract

**Keywords**: Acoustic Metamaterials, Non-Hermitian Acoustics, Thermoacoustic Effect, Active Control, Hatano-Nelson Model, Skin Effect

Conventional acoustic systems are constrained by the principle of reciprocity, hindering the realization of unidirectional wave transmission and topological protection. Among existing technologies, thermoacoustic devices offer passive non-reciprocity with high energy density but lack real-time tunability due to thermal inertia, while purely active electroacoustic control provides a rapid response but encounters bottlenecks regarding high power consumption and signal distortion in large-scale applications. To overcome these limitations, this project proposes an innovative hybrid non-Hermitian acoustic metamaterial architecture based on "thermoacoustic bias and electroacoustic switching."

This study aims to construct a dual-source driven acoustic Hatano-Nelson lattice system by integrating the broadband fluidic gain ($\tau$) of the thermoacoustic effect with the flexible boundary control ($Z_{\mathrm{active}}$) of electroacoustic feedback. Theoretically, the project establishes a precise mapping between continuous acoustic parameters and the hopping amplitudes of discrete topological models based on the transfer matrix method. Experimentally, an FPGA-based pressure-controlled current source is integrated with high-temperature thermoacoustic cores, validating the efficient control strategy of "using heat for energy and electricity for phase."

The project anticipates observing the dynamic non-Hermitian skin effect (NHSE) driven by axial temperature gradients, and aims to develop sub-wavelength low-frequency absorbers and acoustic logic elements capable of millisecond-level switching, opening new energy-efficient technical pathways for topological acoustics and noise control engineering.

## 預期影響

本計畫之執行預期將對產業技術升級、前瞻科技發展與社會環境品質產生顯著影響。在產業應用方面，本研究所開發之次波長熱聲超材料，能有效突破傳統隔音材料受限於質量定律之物理瓶頸，針對離岸風機、大型壓縮機等重機械產生之極低頻噪音提供輕量化且高效率的解決方案，直接提升能源與重工業之環境友善可能性。

在經濟與科技發展面向，本計畫之遂行可實現聲學二極體與電晶體技術，將為抗電磁干擾之聲學邏輯運算、水下通訊網路以及高靈敏度拓樸感測器開闢具高能源效率的技術新藍海，強化台灣在精密聲學工程領域之國際競爭力。此外，藉由跨領域整合熱聲學與數位控制技術，本計畫將培育具備軟硬體系統整合能力之高階科研人才，並且有望透過先進噪音防制技術實質改善民眾居住環境品質。
